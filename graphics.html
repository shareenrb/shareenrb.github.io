Evaluation of web graphics


Site evaluated: Innisfree

Purpose of graphics on this site:


The graphics on the Innisfree home page give off a serene, nature-at-heart feeling to imply its use of natural ingredients, while also maintaining a minimalistic concept to keep the viewer's focus on the products and the product titles. The images that attract the most attention are the ones on the slideshow at the beginning of the page; the images use text that emphasize special sales, with subtitles that provide brief, need-to-know information and backgrounds that compliment the text, rather than steal the attention. There is a bar on the right side of the page used to list Innisfree's weekly best products, and they provide images to show what they look like.

Guidelines for effective use of graphics:

  1. Yes - Innisfree's graphics fit its purpose, organization, and style of the page.
  2. Yes - Large files have been avoided.
  3. Yes - Graphics help guide the viewers' focus to the important contant of the page.
  4. Yes - Repetitive use of overly bright or potentioally "obnoxious" images have been avoided.
  5. No - Using grpahics to convey textual content information has not beeen avoided.
  6. No - Only some parts provide textual equivalent alternatives for graphic content.
  7. Yes - There is sufficient contrast between the text and the background.

Graphic File Types

  1. Image 1: PNG - The colors are flat and there are no gradients or animations.
  2. Image 2: JPEG - There are gradients and many colors.
  3. Image 3: JPEG - The rainbow colored clouds behind the mountain would be supported by this file.
  4. Image 4: PNG - The colors are flat.
  5. Image 5: PNG - The colors are flat and there is a subtle gradient around it.
